Session 4 • Why & what

No tool works alone

Same four tools. Alone they give you pieces; chained they give you a project.

Real academic work isn't “use one AI tool”. It's a chain: research feeds organization, organization feeds the presentation, the presentation needs design — and every link needs verification.

Today's outcome: a mini project that runs through all four tools, plus a short reflection on responsible AI use.
  • Research a topic with Gemini.
  • Organize the knowledge in NotebookLM (with citations).
  • Present it with Gamma.
  • Design a visual asset with Figma.
  • Verify, cite and disclose at every step.

Session 4 • Ethics · visualize it

Verify before you use

The verification loop — run EVERY AI claim through it before it enters your project.

AI sounds confident even when it's wrong. One simple rule: if you haven't checked the source, it doesn't go in your project. NotebookLM shows its sources; for Gemini, YOU find and check them.

Session 4 • Ethics

Citations & academic integrity

do

Work with integrity

Cite every source (author, title, link); rewrite AI text in your own words; keep your prompts — they're your work log; disclose which tools you used where.

don't

Cross these lines

Don't submit AI output unread; don't invent or trust uncited facts; don't upload confidential material; don't present AI work as entirely your own.

Copy this shape: one line per tool saying what it did — and one line for what YOU did.

Session 4 • Hands-on · the mini project

Your mini project — the process

~70 minutes of tool time + 10 minutes for the reflection. Each stage ends with something you can show.

Pick one topic from your branch — e.g. AI in healthcare · renewable microgrids · smart-city traffic — and run it through all four stages. Save your best prompt at every stage — they go in your submission.

This mini project is the assessment for the whole programme. Falling behind? Shrink the artifact, not the verification — a small, cited project beats a big, unchecked one.

Session 4 • Hands-on · reflection

Reflection: responsible AI usage

Your reflection is just this picture in sentences — honest on both sides.
  • Where did AI save time — and where did it mislead you?
  • Which claims did you correct or discard?
  • What did you add that the tools couldn't?
  • Could you defend every slide in an oral exam?

Write 5–8 honest sentences. Honest beats perfect — show you stayed in charge of the tools.
